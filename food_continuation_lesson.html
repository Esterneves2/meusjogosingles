Quick Recall

⏱ 7 minutes · No prep needed
🎓 Teacher Note

Retome o que foi visto na última aula sem avisar. Não mostre as perguntas antes — faça oralmente. Alterne entre Marcella e Caio para manter os dois ativos.

1 Flash Questions — alternating

Ask one to each student quickly, no thinking time:

  • Marcella, tell me 2 foods you like. Go!
  • Caio, tell me 2 foods you don't like. Go!
  • Marcella, what do you usually eat for lunch?
  • Caio, what did you eat yesterday?
  • Marcella, you said you want to try Korean food — why?
  • Caio, you said "nothing I want to try" — really? Tell me one new food you're curious about.

2 Quick Compare — they ask each other

Marcella asks Caio one question from the list below. Caio answers, then asks Marcella one back. 3 rounds, fast.

Do you like ___? What do you eat for ___? Do you want ___ right now?
💬 Goal for this section

Both students talking from minute one. Don't correct — just keep the flow. Praise anything they produce spontaneously.

Taste Words

🎓 Teacher Note

Introduza cada palavra com um exemplo real — use comidas que eles já mencionaram. Não explique gramática. Diga a palavra, dê um exemplo, peça que eles repitam com uma comida de preferência deles.

1 Repeat with a real example

You model it, they copy — then immediately use it with their own food:

  • "Korean food is spicy." → Caio: "Pepper is spicy."
  • "Strawberry juice is sweet." → Marcella: " is sweet."
  • "Pizza is delicious." → Both together
  • "Cow tongue is disgusting — for me!" → They react

Agree or Disagree?

⏱ 12 minutes · High interaction
🎓 Teacher Note

Esta atividade usa a dinâmica de casal — eles naturalmente vão discordar sobre gostos, o que gera fala real e espontânea. Encoraje reações. Se concordarem em tudo, invente uma provocação ("Really? Always?")

🎮 The Food Opinion Game

How it works: You read a statement. Each student says if they AGREE or DISAGREE — and must say why using a taste word. Then they ask each other if they agree.

  • Agree → "Yes! I think so too. [food] is really [word]."
  • Disagree → "No! I don't think so. I think [food] is [word]."

Statements — read one at a time

  1. "Pizza is the most delicious food in the world."
  2. "Salad is healthy, but it's not delicious."
  3. "Coffee is disgusting."
  4. "Soda is too sweet."
  5. "Spicy food is the best food."
  6. "Fast food is heavy, but it's delicious."
  7. "Japanese food is always healthy."
🔁 Push further when they agree fast
  • "Why? Because it's salty? Because it's heavy?"
  • "Caio, do you agree with Marcella?"
  • "Tell me one more reason."

bonus Mini-debate — 2 minutes

Ask them to choose: pizza or burger? Each defends their choice for 30 seconds without stopping. They can use any words they know.

It's delicious because… I don't like ___ because… My favorite is… I think ___ is better

Restaurant 2.0

⏱ 10 minutes · Roleplay upgrade
🎓 Teacher Note

Na aula anterior, fizeram o roleplay básico. Agora é a versão upgraded: tem um problema. O waiter traz a coisa errada, ou não tem o que pediram. Isso força produção oral mais criativa e emergencial — exatamente onde a fluência se desenvolve.

The Setup

They're at a restaurant together (a couple). You play the waiter — but it's a bad day. Things go wrong.

Round 1 Normal Order — 2 min

Script guide
Waiter (you) Hello! What would you like today?
Marcella I want ___. And I want ___ to drink.
Waiter And for you?
Caio I want ___. Is it spicy / sweet / salty?
Waiter Yes / No, it's [taste word].

Round 2 Problem! — 3 min

The waiter brings something wrong. Force them to react and solve it:

  • 🍕 → "Sorry, we don't have pasta today. We have pizza or rice."
  • 🧃 → "Here's your soda!" (but they ordered juice)
  • 🌶️ → "Is this too spicy? Sorry!"

They need to respond — complain, change, ask a question. Any words. No silence longer than 3 seconds!

I don't want this… I want ___ please Excuse me… Can I have ___?

Round 3 Switch roles — 3 min

One of them becomes the waiter. Let them improvise — no script. You can play the difficult customer now.

Final Challenge

⏱ 8 minutes · Speaking + Wrap-up
🎓 Teacher Note

O desafio final cria um produto oral real. Eles vão planejar uma refeição juntos — em inglês. Isso força negociação, escolhas e argumentos. Termine sempre com a Coaching Moment para consolidar a sessão.

🍽️

Plan Your Perfect Dinner

Together, plan a dinner for tonight. You only have 5 minutes. No Portuguese. Every decision must be in English.

The Rules

  • Choose 1 food for Marcella
  • Choose 1 food for Caio
  • Choose 1 drink for both
  • Describe the food using at least 2 taste words
  • One person must say something they don't want

Useful phrases for negotiating

What do you want? Let's have ___ I don't want ___ What about ___? I prefer ___ That's too ___ OK, let's try ___

Present to the teacher

At the end, each student presents the dinner plan — separately — without looking at notes. 30–45 seconds each.

"Our dinner tonight is ___ and ___. I want ___ because it's ___. Caio/Marcella wants ___ because…"

🪞 Coaching Moment — close the class

  • What new word did you use today?
  • What was easier than last class?
  • What do you want to practice more?

Homework (keep it light):

  • Record a 45-second audio: describe your next meal using at least 3 taste words
  • Real-life: next time you eat something, say (out loud) one taste word
