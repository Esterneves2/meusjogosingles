The CVF Loop
10 MIN · MECHANICS

The technique

Every good conversation has a rhythm. Here's the simplest way to keep it going:

C Comment
Say something about the moment		 "This map is brutal."
V Volume
Add a detail about yourself		 "I've died here like six times already."
F Flip
Throw it back to them		 "Have you played this one before?"

Why it works

A conversation isn't a monologue — and it isn't an interrogation either. The CVF loop keeps things balanced. The Flip is the most important part. Without it, you're performing. With it, you're connecting.

Practice — 3 rounds

Use the CVF loop for each situation. Make sure all three parts are there — especially the Flip.

SITUATION 01
Game lobby — waiting for the match

You just got matched with a random. There's 30 seconds before the game starts.

⚡ Must include a Flip question
SITUATION 02
School — someone sits next to you

It's the first week and you don't know this person yet. They just sat down.

⚡ Must include a Volume detail about yourself
SITUATION 03
Discord — text chat becomes a voice call

You've talked in text. Now you're in voice for the first time. Silence.

⚡ Must reference something from a previous text conversation

Dead Conversation
Rescue
10 MIN · SURVIVAL DRILL

The challenge

Your teacher is going to be the worst person to talk to — short answers, no questions back, zero initiative. Your job: keep the conversation alive for 3 minutes. Don't let it die.

Relate
"Same, actually — that happened to me when I was..."
Hypothetical
"Okay but what would you do if..."
Callback
"Wait, you said you play [X] — do you ever..."
Opinion Bait
"Okay genuinely — [X] or [Y]? Be honest."
Round 1

Try without using the tools above. See how long you last and where it breaks down.

Round 2

Same situation — but this time, use at least 2 of the tools. Notice the difference.

After both rounds

  • Which tool felt the most natural to you?
  • Which one felt forced or weird?
  • What's one situation this week where you could use the Hypothetical?

Real Talk Sprint
12 MIN · SIMULATION

How it works

Three situations. Your teacher plays a different character in each one. After each round, take 30 seconds to say out loud what worked.

SITUATION 01 · ~4 MIN
New kid at school

Your teacher is the new student. You want to make them feel welcome — without it sounding like an interview.

⚡ Rule: no "What's your name / where are you from" in the first 3 lines
SITUATION 02 · ~4 MIN
First voice call — after weeks of texting

You've been in the same Discord server for a month. Now you're in voice for the first time. There's that silence.

⚡ Rule: use at least one Callback from something "said in chat before"
SITUATION 03 · ~4 MIN
After winning a ranked game together

You just carried a match with someone you've never played with before. You want to keep this person in your circle.

⚡ Rule: end the conversation with something concrete — without sounding desperate
Between each situation

Name one thing that felt smooth and one thing you want to do differently next time.
