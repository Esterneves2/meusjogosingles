STAGE 2 — TONGUE MAP

MECHANICS · 12 MINUTES

THE TH SOUND — How it actually works

The voiceless TH (as in think, three, through) is produced like this:

UPPER TEETH
─────────────
TONGUE TIP → | ← touch lightly
─────────────
LOWER TEETH
Air flows OUT between tongue tip and upper teeth. No voice. No friction buzz.

THE 3 MISTAKES — and what to fix

MISTAKE 1 — "F" SUBSTITUTION

Saying free instead of three. The tongue is not touching the teeth — air escapes through the lip-teeth friction instead. Fix: push your tongue forward until it touches the upper teeth.

MISTAKE 2 — "T" SUBSTITUTION

Saying tink instead of think. The tongue is hitting the roof of the mouth. Fix: the tongue must stay near the teeth, not the palate. Air must flow — not stop.

MISTAKE 3 — TOO MUCH FRICTION

Overcompensating by pressing the tongue too hard, creating a buzzing sound. Fix: light contact only. The tongue should barely graze the teeth. Think of gently blowing air out.

THE R SOUND — For someone with diction issues in PT-BR too

Your R difficulty is partly muscular — the same articulatory imprecision that affects PT-BR also shows up in English. In English the R is retroflex: the tongue curls back slightly, it never touches anything, and the lips round very slightly.

  • Tongue tip points up and slightly back — no contact
  • Middle of tongue is raised
  • Lips relax into a slight rounding
  • The sound comes from the throat shaping, not tongue friction
DRILL — TONGUE PLACEMENT

Put your tongue between your teeth. Blow air slowly. You should feel air flowing over the tongue tip. Do this 5 times, then add voice: "ththththth".

This is the exact position for voiceless TH. Lock this feeling in.
DRILL — R ISOLATION

Say "uh" — now curl your tongue back slightly without touching anything. Feel the sound shift to an R. Hold it: "urrrrr". Now say: run, red, really, rather, through.

The R should feel like a shape, not a movement.
